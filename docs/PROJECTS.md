## üìã Proposal Requirements

To submit a project for CrypTrans funding, proposals must include:

### 1. Vision & Impact
- **Problem statement:** What existential challenge does this address?
- **Solution overview:** How does this advance transhumanism?
- **Impact metrics:** Lives saved/extended, capabilities unlocked

### 2. Technical Approach
- **Scientific basis:** Peer-reviewed research supporting feasibility
- **Technical milestones:** Concrete deliverables per year
- **Risk assessment:** Technical challenges and mitigation strategies

### 3. Team & Credentials
- **Principal investigators:** PhDs, track record in field
- **Advisory board:** Domain experts validating approach
- **Institutional backing:** University/company partnerships

### 4. Budget & Timeline
- **Detailed budget:** Line-item breakdown
- **Funding tranches:** Multi-year release schedule
- **Success metrics:** Quantifiable KPIs per milestone

### 5. Ethical Considerations
- **Safety protocols:** How risks are managed
- **Regulatory compliance:** FDA, EPA, international agreements
- **Dual-use concerns:** Preventing malicious applications

### 6. Open Source Commitment
- **Code/data release:** Timeline for public availability
- **Publication plan:** Peer-reviewed papers
- **Community engagement:** Open development model

## üéØ Evaluation Criteria

CrypTrans DAO evaluates proposals on:

1. **Alignment with Mission** (30%)
   - Does it advance cryonics, longevity, space, AI safety, or post-scarcity?

2. **Technical Feasibility** (25%)
   - Is the science sound? Are milestones achievable?

3. **Team Capability** (20%)
   - Does the team have expertise and track record?

4. **Impact Potential** (15%)
   - Could this save millions of lives or unlock new capabilities?

5. **Timeline Realism** (10%)
   - Are milestones and budget realistic?
